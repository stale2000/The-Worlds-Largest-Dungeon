LONGTAIL
Longtail is a wererat sorcerer with a quick mind, cha-
risma, and grand ambitions. With research and the 
aid of his imp familiar Achsyyx, Longtail uncovered

Longtail‚Äôs endless charm allowed him to bring

orcs, and even an ogre rallied to his side.
Fueled by the promise of treasure and led by liz-

entrance, they began exploring the area searching 
for the tomb‚Äôs fabled wealth. Longtail himself, guid-

At first, empty-handed, the humanoids were 
eager to exploit their new home, greeting Long-

Things did not go as planned. A wave of extraplanar

Random Encounters
The release of the extraplanar beasts has thrown this 
entire Region into chaos. Whereas before, there were 
clear areas under the influence of a particular group 
of humanoids, the resulting chaos means that there 
are no longer any actual areas of control. Between the 
hunting beasts and the scared, lost humanoids, there 
is plenty of motion and confusion in this dungeon. As 
a result, a party traveling through this Region is just 
as likely to encounter a reptilian as they are an orc or 
an extraplanar beast.
Rather than checking at some specific time interval 
for random encounters, the GM should use this table 
whenever they deem appropriate. Remember that 
despite the number of Rooms in this Region, there is 
not a lot of distance between most Rooms and areas 
and sound carries well in such an environment. If the 
action is slow ‚Äî for instance, the PCs are taking their 
time searching through the rubble in a Room or are 
being extra cautious as they move down a hall ‚Äî the 
events from this table will liven things up.
Another way to use the chart is to add a random 
element to the encounters described in the various 
Rooms. Many of the listed random encounters add 
an interesting twist to the otherwise standard com-
bat scenarios inevitable in a dungeon of this size. 
After all, the noise of combat is bound to attract 
some interest.
A final option for this table is if the PCs return to 
areas or Rooms where they have already been. Since 
the dungeon is in constant flux, it is likely that some-
thing other than the original inhabitants now occupy 
the Room. A particularly bold GM could use this 
table to add the occasional encounter to an otherwise 
empty Room.

A1 . ENTRANCE 
This is the last chance the PCs have to rest and reflect 
before entering the dungeon. Nothing else enters it 
as long as the PCs are in the entrance chamber. An 
inspection of the passageway as it approaches the 
‚Äòbarrier‚Äô reveals that it is absolutely featureless, devoid 
of markings, symbols, or even any sign of use.
As you move through the passageway, the edge of 
the tunnel disappears into a blackness that lies just 
beyond. It is not a wall barring entry but an inde-
scribable void. Darkness and light meet in a perfect 
duality. Peering cautiously into the darkness for 
signs of movement, you see none. There is a still-
ness in the air. No breeze in front of you. Nothing. 
Your ears detect only the breathing of your fellow 

31
adventurers. The darkness betrays nothing of what 
lies beyond.
Initial Attitude: N/A 
Encounter: The passageway ends in an opaque 
magical field. This field prevents all vision, includ-
ing darkvision, as well as all sound from passing 
through from one side to the other in either direc-
tion. In addition, the magical field has an unusual 
and permanent force effect that allows creatures 
and items to enter the dungeon without difficulty 
but absolutely prevents them from leaving.
Opaque Door Barrier: Permanent, one-way wall of 
force. Cannot be dispelled or disintegrated, and no 
sound can pass through it.
If the PCs want to enter the dungeon, they 
simply must take that leap of faith and step beyond 
the door. Cautious PCs may stick only a hand or an 
object through, but they find that when they try to 
withdraw the hand or object, they cannot because 
the barrier prevents the return movement. No 
amount of pulling brings the hand or object back 
through the barrier, although if enough force is 
used, the object could undoubtedly be broken or 
the hand severed.
Encounter Condition: Safe
Tactics: Subtly encourage the PCs to prepare them-
selves by healing, resting, and readying equipment 
and weapons, etc., prior to having them enter the 
dungeon if they need it. Nothing bothers them as 
long as they are in this Room, and they can take all 
the time they need to make sure they are in tip-
top shape. 
Treasure: N/A 
Scaling: To increase the challenge of this encounter, 
do not indicate a barrier has been passed. Simply 
let the PCs walk through an invisible force field 
unknowingly. To decrease the challenge of this 
encounter, give the PCs a hint that this barrier is 
one-way, or simply ignore this effect completely. 
Allow the PCs to come and go as they please. This 
eliminates a lot of the dread of the dungeon but 
does give back one more gaming option to players 
who demand complete agency.
A2-A20 . ORC STRONGHOLD
Rooms A2 through A20 make up the former orc 
stronghold within this Region. The orcs chose the 
area closest to the entrance for defensive reasons and 
launched all of their search expeditions from here. 
While this portion of the dungeon is defensively 
strong, these same qualities limit the ability of the 
orcs to explore the other parts of the dungeon.
As the conflict with the reptilians escalated, the 
orcs‚Äô leader, a barbarian named Orghar, decided that 
it was time to force a decision. Marshaling the orcs, 
he led them in an all-or-nothing assault on the reptil-
ians throughout the dungeon.
Since launching the attack, the orcs have aban-
doned this section. Although they planned to return 
and establish a garrison to guard the entrance once 
they were victorious, none returned. Various groups 
established themselves throughout the Region but 
have been displaced since.
